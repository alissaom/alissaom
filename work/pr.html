    Design Thinking Exchange

    FSU CCI + Universidad de Sagrado Corazon

    International Exchange Design Thinking Program

    Overview

    Through the guidance of Christine Nieves, Entrepreneur in Residence for CCI, Sagrado Corazón professors, and NEEUKO innovators, our group was able to ideate a technology-based start-up within 48 hours. By presenting our business idea to professors, students, investors, and leaders across the Santurce, P.R. community, we were able to modify and further develop our idea to be more applicable for the local community.

    The Story

    In collaboration with La Universidad del Sagrado Corazón, an interdisciplinary group of Florida State Students travelled to Puerto Rico to participate in a week-long innovation program that used Design Thinking methodology to ideate and propose a start-up. Our team, composed of STARS (Students & Technology in Academia, Research, and Service) and a Social Science student specializing in Social Entrepreneurship, worked with NEEUKO (Sagrado Corazon's Collaborative Innovation Center) innovators in an effort to create solutions across geographical, linguistic, and cultural differences.

    In addition to our entrepreneurial work, our team proposed the STARS leadership model to the La Universidad del Sagrado Corazón's President and Provost. We also presented the STARS model to the Puerto Rico Science, Technology & Research Trust in hopes that the model could help with their ultimate goal of stimulating innovation, technology commercialization and the creation of high-technology jobs in Puerto Rico.

    Cultural Immersion

    Along with our international exchange program, we got a chance to better understand the Puerto Rican community as well as it's local tech scene. We toured El Cano Martin. We had to honor to meet with Vice President of Caribnet, the fastest internet service provider of the Caribbean. I was able to hike for the first time, along a crazy talented team, in a rainforest - El Yunque National Forest.

    Recognition + Thanks

    We are very thankful to the College of Communication and Information at Florida State University and Christine Nieves for creating this opportunity. If it were not for Universidad de Sagrado Corazon and Nueeko, we would not have had the mentors, collaboration space, and community feedback. Thank you all for allowing us to partake in such an amazing experience that will last a lifetime.
